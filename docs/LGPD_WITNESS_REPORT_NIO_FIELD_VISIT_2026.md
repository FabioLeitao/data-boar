## Executive summary

In **April 2026**, during a **door-to-door visit** by **uniformed representatives** identifying as employees of **NIO** (a telecommunications / internet access provider) in a **residential area of Niterói**, Rio de Janeiro metropolitan region, Brazil, the author was about to subscribe to a service when concerns arose about **personal data handling**, in particular **photography of the front of the residence**, **address (CEP) lookup practices**, and **clarity on deletion / accountability** (including **DPO** visibility).

The interaction remained **polite on both sides**; the report does **not** allege theft, fraud, or impersonation. The concern is **governance and LGPD-aligned practices** (transparency, purpose limitation, data minimization, accountability), and the **right of data subjects to clear answers** when sensitive operations (e.g., images tied to an address) are involved.

This document is published in the **Data Boar** repository as **open documentation** so neighbors and other consumers can **compare notes**, **exercise rights**, and **use official channels** (ANPD, ANATEL, and, when appropriate, law enforcement) without relying on informal chat alone.

## Chronology (facts as observed)

1. Representatives presented as **NIO** staff, **uniformed**, with **visible ID badges**, offering **internet service** in the neighborhood.
2. Technical questions about the service received **clear answers**; the author was **close to subscribing** (including discussing installation date).
3. The author observed **photography of the front of the residence** and asked **why** it was taken and **how** it would be used, stored, shared, and **deleted** if necessary.
4. Explanations referenced **internal system behavior** (e.g., **CEP** not found as expected). The author asked for assurance that **no unnecessary personal data** would be retained and that **deletion** could be **demonstrated** where applicable.
5. The conversation became **strained** when the author insisted on **data-subject rights** and **accountability**; the author **did not** intend to offend individuals, but to **assert statutory expectations** under **LGPD**.
6. The author **did not complete** the subscription **until** governance questions were satisfactorily addressed.

## LGPD angles relevant to this case (general)

- **Transparency and purpose:** Processing must be **explained** in an accessible way; photography of a façade linked to an address can be **personal data** in context.
- **Minimization:** Collect **only** what is **necessary** for the stated purpose (e.g., feasibility / installation planning).
- **Accountability:** Organizations should be able to point to **policies**, **records**, and **responsible roles** (including **DPO** where required).
- **Data subject rights:** Including **access**, **correction**, **anonymization**, **deletion**, **portability**, and information on **sharing** — subject to legal limits.

Official interpretation and enforcement belong to **ANPD** and the courts.

## If you already signed during a visit

1. Request **written** information on **processing**, **legal basis**, **retention**, and **DPO contact** (where applicable).
2. Request **evidence** of **deletion** of any **non-essential** personal data, including **images**, if they are not required for service delivery.
3. If responses are inadequate, use **ANPD** / **ANATEL** channels and consider **legal counsel** before escalating.
